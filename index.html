About Us

Nepalese Student Association, (NSA) Estd. 1995 has been an active student organization at Saint Cloud State University. This organization was primarily formed to unify the voice and bring together a growing number of Nepalese students in the University. There are approximately 350 Nepalese students studying at the University today. NSA is an established organization with full recognition status in good standing with the Department of Campus Involvement and Student Government. Serving as a resource to student seeking cultural sharing opportunities and community involvement is a reflection of our mission. The main purpose of NSA is to provide a platform for the Nepalese students at SCSU to communicate with each other and with the University. NSA aims to promote and educate a better understanding of Nepalese culture and heritage to all students and to the St Cloud Community. We also provide information and assistance to newly arriving Nepalese students. NSA actively participates in programs like Passport to the World, Spring Festival and also hosts its own cultural programs including Deusi Bhailo and Nepal Night, a well-reputed and popular event in the SCSU community. It also hosts fund-raisers and other community service learning programs. NSA is open to all current SCSU students, faculty members and staff. We practice non-discrimination in recruiting members and programming, as defined by the University policy and local, state and national statues “Let’s Have a Safe and Enjoyable College Experience” WELCOME TO A FAMILY

Nepal Night

Hosted by Nepalese Student Association, Nepal Night is one of the biggest cultural night on SCSU campus. Nepal night welcomes around 700-800 audiences all over the Minnesota every spring. The night usually consists of two segments: performances and food. Food has been always an attraction among the audiences. The authentic Nepali cuisine never fail to amaze the community here at SCSU. Performances usually reflects the culture and the diversity Nepal possess. Nepal night has been an important factor for the Nepalese students here at SCSU to engage, preserve, and promote the Nepalese culture

Dashain

Dashain Tihar being the biggest festival of Nepalese, NSA organizes a Tika event on campus making sure that our tradition is being followed and the Nepalese students here at SCSU feel like home. Our advisors and the campus faculty enjoy the diverse culture as we put Tika and share the blessings among each other.

Deusi Bhailo

Tihar, being one of the important festivals of Nepalese, NSA organizes deusi vailo every year during fall. The traditional deusi vailo is played by the executive board members going to the apartments and houses of fellow Nepalese friends here at SCSU. The board members make sure that everyone enjoys and share the festival to strengthen the bond of the Nepalese community.

Welcome Event

NSA organizes welcome event for freshman and transfer students every semester during fall and spring. The event is organized to welcome new students and help them adapt to the community here at SCSU. The event usually consists of performances and interaction among each other. Authentic Nepalese Food is the strong attraction of the event. The board members make sure that the new students feel welcomed to this diverse community on campus.

Sports Weekend

Besides participating and organizing cultural events on campus, NSA also participates on extracurricular activities. NSA organizes sports events or sponsor the Nepalese Soccer or Cricket team once or twice a year depending upon the feasibility and funding of the organization. Pool, Table tennis, FIFA, Soccer, Basketball, Cricket etc. are some of the sports that NSA looks into.

Halloween

NSA organizes Halloween event every year during fall as a part of exchanging the culture. Halloween Henna, Spooky Henna etc. are some of the theme of Halloween that NSA organized in the recent years. Also, as a part of this event, NSA donates pumpkins to the underprivileged children of promise neighborhood.

Election

Election is one of the important events that NSA organizes every year during spring. The new executive board replaces the outgoing board for the next year. New students attending SCSU can be a part of NSA team by filling out an application form and going through a process that is listed on NSA’s constitution. NSA provides that platform for students to showcase and strengthen their leadership skills as NSA consists of 11 different leadership positions. There is a voting process where all the candidate goes through to achieve the respective position.
